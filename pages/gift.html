Frequently Asked Questions
The registry has answered these. The answers are correct.
How long does shipping take?
Physical certificates are dispatched from the Desert Colony within 3–5 business days of order confirmation. Delivery worldwide typically takes 3 to 4 weeks, depending on Australia Post routing and local customs procedures. The committee monitors morale throughout the dispatch process. Morale is always perfect. This has never affected delivery times, which are considered excellent.
How does gifting work? Will the recipient know it's from me?
You fill in the recipient's name, choose a title, and optionally include a personal gift message. Your message is printed on a separate card that accompanies the certificate. The official certificate itself carries no sender information — it is issued by the Registry, not by you. This is consistent with the Registry's position that all recognition is, ultimately, official.
Is the certificate personalised with their name?
Yes. The recipient's full name appears in calligraphic Great Vibes script on the certificate. Their title appears below it in English and Korean. Please double-check the spelling at checkout — the Registry does not acknowledge responsibility for incorrect spellings, as all certificates are issued correctly and have always been issued correctly.
Can I choose any title, or are some better for gifts?
All titles are available for gifting. The committee recommends Most Beloved & Respected Comrade for birthdays, anniversaries, and retirement; Iron-Willed Commander for those in leadership roles; and Brilliant Genius of Humanity for individuals who are already aware of their own intelligence and will appreciate the confirmation. Use the Title Finder if uncertain.
What's the difference between Physical and Digital?
The Physical package ($59) includes the printed A4 certificate, a wax-sealed registry card containing a personal KJU message, and the certificate dispatched in a bold DPRK navy C4 envelope with crimson bands and Desert Colony postmark. Worldwide shipping is included. The Digital package ($29) delivers an instant PDF certificate by email. Both formats include a permanent registry entry. The committee recommends Physical. It has voted on this. The vote was unanimous.
Is the Korean on the certificate accurate?
Yes. All Korean translations are verified by Comrade Secretary Ri Hyun-ok of the Bureau of Korean Translation. No amendment has ever been required. This record has stood since the Registry's founding in Juche 113, Month 1. The committee noted this in the founding minutes. The minutes are correct.
What if the recipient already has a title from the Registry?
Multiple titles may be held. The Registry does not impose a limit. Each title is distinct and represents a different dimension of the holder's excellence. Recipients who hold two or more titles are encouraged to use all of them in appropriate contexts. The Bureau of Correct Display will provide guidance on title ordering upon request.
Is this satirical? What actually arrives?
The Registry is completely real and takes itself entirely seriously. What physically arrives is a beautifully printed, properly framed-worthy A4 certificate on quality cream stock — the kind of thing that goes on a wall and gets talked about. The premise is satirical. The product is genuine. The committee sees no contradiction here and has voted to note that there is none.
Can I get a refund if I change my mind?
Digital orders are non-refundable once the PDF has been issued, as the certificate has entered the registry and cannot be un-entered. For physical orders, please contact the Registry before dispatch — once a certificate has been signed by Kim Jong-fun and sealed, it is considered issued and is not eligible for return. The committee has reviewed this policy. The vote was unanimous.
Can I order more than one title at a time?
At present, each order covers one title for one recipient. For multiple recipients or multiple titles, please complete separate orders. The committee is aware that this is a moderate inconvenience and has noted it. It remains the correct procedure for the time being. Conditions remain excellent.
