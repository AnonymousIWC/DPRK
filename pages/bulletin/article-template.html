Bulletin No. 1 Registry Dispatch
The Registry Is Now Operational. Conditions Are Excellent. There Is Nothing Further to Report.
Juche 113, Month 1 Issued by the Supreme Title Registry · Desert Colony, Australia · 29°S 131°E
Opening Declaration

By Supreme Decree of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea, the DPRK Honorary Title Registry is hereby declared operational. The Registry has been established for the purpose of extending the unique honour of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea's historic honorary title system to the global civilian population — an act of supreme magnanimity unprecedented in the history of voluntary institutions operating from the geographic centre of Australia. Operations commenced on Month 1, Juche 113. Conditions are excellent. This bulletin confirms these facts.

The purpose of this founding dispatch is to place on the permanent record the circumstances of the Registry's establishment, the composition of its leadership, the status of its facilities, and the nature of its mission. All of these things are correct. They have been reviewed. The review was conducted by the same committee that reviewed all previous matters. The committee found no errors. This is consistent with every prior review.

The Approvals Committee

The Approvals Committee convened its inaugural meeting on the morning of Month 1 at the Desert Colony operational headquarters. All six committee members were present. The agenda comprised seven items. All seven items were resolved by unanimous vote. The vote on Item 4, concerning the correct wax temperature for registry seal application, proceeded with particular efficiency. Comrade Director Pak Sung-jin, Chair of the Bureau of Title Verification and Correctness, confirmed that all procedural foundations were correct and had always been correct. The committee accepted this confirmation unanimously. The meeting concluded at 11:47 a.m. local time. Conditions at 11:47 a.m. were excellent.

The committee comprises six members: Comrade Director Pak Sung-jin (Bureau of Title Verification and Correctness), Comrade Secretary Ri Hyun-ok (Korean Translation — no amendment has ever been required), Comrade Director Choe Kwang-sop (Bureau of Recipient Happiness), Comrade Director Kim Yong-ae (Bureau of Correct Display), Comrade Director O Yong-sik (Desert Colony Operations), and Comrade Senior Adviser Jang Tae-un (Emeritus, who declined retirement on ideological grounds and whose continued presence the committee considers an asset). The committee meets quarterly. Its votes are unanimous. They are always unanimous.

The Supreme Registrar

Kim Jong-un was appointed Supreme Registrar by unanimous committee resolution on the same morning. Born Juche 82 under remarkable circumstances — a double rainbow was observed over the place of birth, and birds arranged themselves in a formation that observers described as meaningful — Kim Jong-un brings to the role a distinguished personal history and an unsurpassed commitment to ceremony, calligraphy, and the correct application of wax. Upon receiving the appointment, Kim Jong-un bowed for seventeen seconds. This duration has been entered into the Registry's permanent record as the longest bow in Registry history and, it must be noted, the only bow in Registry history. It was, in the committee's assessment, performed correctly.

"The Registry has been established. Titles are available. Certificates will be dispatched worldwide. This is correct." — Kim Jong-un, Supreme Registrar · Juche 113, Month 1
The Desert Colony

The Desert Colony was established at coordinates 29°S 131°E — the precise geographic centre of Australia, selected by the committee following a forty-five-minute review of satellite cartography and one brief discussion about whether Australia had a centre. It does. The committee confirmed this on the third ballot. The Desert Colony operates at full capacity. It is staffed entirely by volunteers. No volunteer has resigned. No volunteer has expressed dissatisfaction. All worker correspondence is reviewed by Comrade Director O Yong-sik before dispatch. This review is a standard operational procedure and is in no way indicative of any concern about worker correspondence. There are no concerns. Conditions are excellent.

The potato situation has been assessed and confirmed as abundant. The committee noted this finding in the founding minutes. It is anticipated that future quarterly reports will confirm continued abundance. The committee will be pleased to report this.

Operational Status

The Registry's operational systems are fully functional. The certificate production facility is operational. The worldwide postage infrastructure is engaged. Australia Post has been informed. The online registry database has received its first entry, which has been verified as correct. The wax seal supply has been assessed as adequate for the foreseeable future. All systems are operating at record levels. This is the first record. It is already being exceeded.

Certificates will be issued on 200gsm cream stock. Each certificate will bear the name of the recipient in calligraphic script, the title in full in both English and 조선어, the state emblem, the signature of Kim Jong-un as Supreme Registrar, and two official seals: the Registry Seal and the Juche Calendar Seal. The certificate will be dispatched in a C4 navy envelope with crimson bands and the Desert Colony postmark. Worldwide shipping is included. There are no additional fees. There are no exceptions.

The Registry's mission is the issuance of honorary titles of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea to the global civilian population. Titles issued by this Registry are permanent. They cannot be rescinded, disputed, or reviewed. The Registry does not acknowledge the possibility of error in the title issuance process. Recipients of Registry titles are encouraged to use their title at all times and in all appropriate contexts. The Bureau of Correct Display will issue guidance on appropriate contexts in a forthcoming bulletin. That guidance will also be correct.

This bulletin is the first bulletin ever issued by the Registry. It is correct. The Registry has never issued an incorrect bulletin. This record begins now and will be maintained indefinitely. The committee has noted this commitment in the founding minutes. The vote was unanimous.

Issued under the authority of the Supreme Title Registry Desert Colony, Australia · Juche 113, Month 1
