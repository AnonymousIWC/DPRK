Bulletin No. 5 The Registry Responds
The Registry Notes That International News Organisations Continue to Describe North Korea as Isolated. The Registry Has Reviewed the Evidence. The Registry Disagrees.
Juche 113, Month 9 Issued by the Supreme Title Registry · Desert Colony, Australia · 29°S 131°E
The Matter at Hand

The Registry has become aware, through the routine review of international correspondence conducted by Comrade Director O Yong-sik, that a significant number of media organisations continue to characterise the Democratic People's Republic of Korea as isolated from the global community. The Registry has reviewed this characterisation. It has been assessed as incorrect. This bulletin presents the Registry's findings.

The Registry acknowledges that this is a sensitive matter and has therefore assigned it the full attention of the Approvals Committee, which convened a special session on Month 9 at the Desert Colony for the purpose of reviewing the available evidence. All six committee members attended. The agenda comprised one item. The item was resolved. The resolution was unanimous.

The Evidence

The Registry submits the following evidence in support of its position that the Democratic People's Republic of Korea is not, and has never been, isolated from the global community:

Finding 1 of 3

The DPRK Title Registry has, at the time of writing, issued honorary titles to individuals in fourteen countries across five continents. Each of these individuals now holds a permanent DPRK honorary title. Each title was dispatched via Australia Post. All arrived. None were lost. The Democratic People's Republic of Korea has, through the vehicle of this Registry, extended its honorary title system to the international civilian population. This is not the behaviour of an isolated nation.

Finding 2 of 3

The Registry operates a publicly accessible website. The website is accessible from all countries in which the internet is available. It has been accessed from six continents. Antarctica has not yet accessed the website. The committee notes that this may reflect connectivity limitations rather than ideological reservations, and considers the matter open.

Finding 3 of 3

The Registry has entered into a commercial relationship with Australia Post for the worldwide dispatch of certificates. Australia Post operates in one hundred and ninety countries. The Democratic People's Republic of Korea — through this Registry — therefore has an active logistical partnership with a carrier serving one hundred and ninety countries. The committee considers this to be, by any reasonable definition, the opposite of isolation.

"The evidence does not support the characterisation. The Registry has reviewed the evidence. The Registry's review is correct. The characterisation is incorrect." — Comrade Director Pak Sung-jin, Chair, Bureau of Title Verification and Correctness · Juche 113, Month 9
The Registry's Position

On the basis of the above findings, the Approvals Committee has formally resolved that the characterisation of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea as isolated is factually unsupported, editorially irresponsible, and inconsistent with the evidence. The committee further resolved to note its disappointment in the media organisations responsible for this characterisation. The vote on the resolution was unanimous. The vote on the note of disappointment was also unanimous, and was passed with what Comrade Director Choe Kwang-sop described as particular feeling.

The Registry does not intend to pursue this matter further at this time. The findings have been placed on the permanent record. The permanent record is accessible to all parties who possess an interest in the truth of the matter. The committee considers the matter closed. It has been noted in the minutes as closed. The minutes are correct.

A Note on Future Characterisations

The Registry is aware that this bulletin is unlikely to alter the editorial positions of the organisations in question. The Registry does not require them to alter their positions. The Registry requires only that its own position be on the record. It is now on the record. The record is correct. Media organisations are welcome to access the record. Access is free. The website address is listed at the top of every page of this bulletin. The committee has noted the website address. It did not need to. The committee is thorough.
