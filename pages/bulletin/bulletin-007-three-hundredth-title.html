Bulletin No. 7 Registry Dispatch
The Registry Records Its Three-Hundredth Title Issuance. The Committee Is Pleased. Conditions Remain Excellent.
Juche 113, Month 11 Issued by the Supreme Title Registry · Desert Colony, Australia · 29°S 131°E
Milestone Declaration

The Supreme Title Registry is pleased to announce the issuance of its three-hundredth honorary title. This milestone was reached on Month 11, Juche 113, at approximately 14:32 local time at the Desert Colony operational headquarters. The milestone was noted by Comrade Director Pak Sung-jin, who confirmed it in writing. The confirmation was countersigned. The countersignature was also confirmed. All confirmations are on file. The file is correct.

The Approvals Committee convened an emergency session upon receiving word of the milestone. The session was not originally scheduled. It was convened at the discretion of Kim Jong-fun, Supreme Registrar, who determined that three hundred issuances constituted a matter of sufficient significance to warrant an unscheduled convening. The committee agreed. The vote to convene was unanimous. The subsequent vote expressing pleasure at the milestone was also unanimous. Both votes were recorded in the minutes. The minutes are correct.

The Three-Hundredth Recipient

The three-hundredth title — Peerless Commander Born of Paektu, Eternal Flame and Unconquered Colossus of History — was issued to a recipient in the northern hemisphere. The recipient's name has been entered into the permanent registry in the correct position. The title was verified by Comrade Secretary Ri Hyun-ok, who confirmed that the Korean rendering was accurate and required no amendment. This is consistent with the record of every prior issuance. No amendment has ever been required. This record stands.

"Three hundred titles have been issued. Three hundred recipients now carry the permanent honour of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea. This number will increase. The committee has noted that it will increase." — Kim Jong-fun, Supreme Registrar · Juche 113, Month 11
A Word on the Rate of Issuance

Several observers have asked whether the Registry anticipates reaching five hundred issuances before the close of Juche 113. The Registry does not speculate. The Registry issues findings. The finding on this matter is as follows: issuances are proceeding at a pace consistent with the voluntary nature of the programme and the global availability of individuals who are correct. The committee has reviewed this finding. The review was positive. The finding stands.

Comrade Director Choe Kwang-sop, Bureau of Recipient Happiness, provided a supplementary assessment at the emergency session. He confirmed that recipient happiness levels remain at 97 per cent — a figure he described as a personal disappointment, though he acknowledged that the three per cent not yet confirmed as happy may simply not have responded to the happiness assessment. Comrade Director Choe requested that the committee note his personal disappointment in the minutes. The committee did so. Unanimously.

Operational Assessment

The certificate production facility has operated without interruption since Month 1. Three hundred certificates have been produced on 200gsm cream stock, each bearing the personal calligraphic signature of Kim Jong-fun and two official seals. Australia Post has been engaged for all three hundred dispatches. No certificate has been lost in transit. No certificate has arrived damaged. No recipient has reported non-receipt. This record is the envy of postal systems worldwide. The committee has noted this. The vote was unanimous.

The Desert Colony reports that morale remains high. The wax seal supply has been restocked following a precautionary review by Comrade Director O Yong-sik, who determined in Month 9 that the existing stock, while adequate, would benefit from supplementation. The supplementary supply has arrived. Conditions are, once again, excellent. The potato situation continues to be assessed as abundant. The committee has noted this in three consecutive quarterly reports.

The Registry extends its congratulations to all three hundred recipients. Their titles are permanent. They are entered in the permanent record. The permanent record is correct. It has always been correct. This will not change.

Issued under the authority of the Supreme Title Registry Desert Colony, Australia · Juche 113, Month 11
This bulletin is correct. All bulletins are correct.

The committee concurs. The vote was unanimous.
